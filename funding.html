Funding Sources

Funding strategy across public, private, and philanthropic sources.

Summit Grant Services helps organizations understand the funding landscape, identify realistic opportunities, and pursue the right mix of government, foundation, corporate, and strategic funding sources.

We help clients navigate the full funding ecosystem.

Strong funding strategies rarely depend on a single source. Summit helps clients evaluate opportunities across multiple funder types and build a practical approach based on eligibility, timing, competitiveness, capacity, and long-term fit.

Federal Grants

Support identifying, interpreting, and pursuing competitive and formula-based federal grant opportunities across U.S. government agencies, including complex NOFOs, registrations, attachments, budgets, and compliance requirements.

State Grants

Research and proposal support for state-administered funding opportunities, including agency grants, legislative appropriations, capital programs, workforce funds, health and human services grants, and rural development initiatives.

State Pass-Through Funding

Assistance with opportunities where federal funds flow through state agencies, regional entities, councils of government, departments, authorities, or other intermediary organizations.

Local Government Funding

Support for funding opportunities issued by counties, cities, regional planning bodies, local authorities, community development entities, and public-sector partnership programs.

Family Foundations

Research, relationship strategy, letters of inquiry, proposals, and positioning support for private and family foundations aligned with mission, geography, program area, or community need.

Corporate Foundations

Support identifying and pursuing corporate foundation opportunities, community investment programs, sponsorship-aligned funding, employee giving programs, and mission-aligned corporate philanthropy.

High Net Worth Philanthropy

Strategic support for organizations approaching major donors, donor-advised funds, private philanthropists, and high-capacity individuals where the case for impact must be clear and compelling.

Public-Private Partnerships

Support for complex projects that require blended funding, partner coordination, government alignment, private investment, philanthropic support, or multi-stakeholder implementation.

Innovation & Non-Dilutive Funding

Assistance for companies, research teams, and institutions exploring non-dilutive capital, technology demonstration funding, commercialization programs, and mission-aligned public funding.

Not every funding opportunity is worth pursuing.

One of the most valuable parts of funding strategy is knowing when to move forward — and when not to. Summit helps clients evaluate opportunity fit before investing significant time and organizational capacity.

Eligibility

We review applicant eligibility, geography, entity type, project type, match requirements, partnership requirements, and threshold criteria before recommending a pursuit.

Competitiveness

We consider scoring criteria, funder priorities, prior awards, project readiness, partnerships, outcomes, budget alignment, and whether the opportunity is realistically winnable.

Organizational Capacity

We help clients assess the staff time, documentation, reporting, compliance, financial management, and implementation obligations that come with each opportunity.

Funding strategy should match the project.

Different projects require different funding pathways. Summit helps clients align the opportunity with the real purpose, scale, timing, and readiness of the project.

Program Funding

Grants and philanthropic support for service delivery, program expansion, pilot projects, outreach, direct services, and community-based initiatives.

Capital & Infrastructure

Funding strategies for facilities, equipment, construction, renovation, infrastructure, transportation, broadband, energy, water, and other capital-intensive projects.

Capacity Building

Support for staffing, planning, evaluation, systems development, technology, organizational development, partnerships, and long-term sustainability.

Research & Demonstration

Funding for technical, scientific, innovation, feasibility, prototype, pilot, demonstration, and evidence-building projects.

Planning & Strategy

Support for needs assessments, strategic plans, feasibility studies, community plans, environmental reviews, engineering, and implementation planning.

Evaluation & Impact

Funding and advisory support for performance measurement, program evaluation, data systems, impact reporting, and outcome-based funding models.

Our funding research process.

Summit’s research process is designed to produce useful decisions, not just long lists of grants.

1. Understand the client and project

We review the organization, goals, geography, entity type, funding need, timeline, and readiness.

2. Search across relevant funding sources

We examine government, foundation, corporate, philanthropic, and specialized funding channels.

3. Screen for eligibility and fit

We assess whether the opportunity truly fits the client, project, budget, timing, and compliance capacity.

4. Prioritize the strongest opportunities

We help clients focus on the opportunities most likely to be realistic, strategic, and worth pursuing.

5. Build an actionable funding pipeline

We organize opportunities by deadline, fit, next steps, required materials, and recommended pursuit strategy.
